Down a narrow alleyway, nestled between a laundromat and a defunct tailor shop, sat a vending machine unlike any other. It dispensed no snacks or sodas, but rather small, smooth river stones, each engraved with a single, prophetic word. For fifty cents, you could receive a personalized, albeit cryptic, peek into your immediate future. Today's acquisition, for a weary software developer named Julian, was the word "TULIP." He stood there, turning the stone over in his palm, completely baffled. The machine's only other feature was a faint, buzzing sound that seemed to be the recorded laughter of a hundred tiny children. Julian decided to forgo his lunch break and spend the afternoon searching for any possible, relevant meaning for a springtime flower in his mid-October life.
